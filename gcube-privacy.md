
# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** April 8, 2026

GCube Launcher ("the app") is a launcher application for Android. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app accesses, how that data is used, and how it is handled.

## 1. Developer and contact

Developer / privacy contact: `ludeveloper97-dev`

## 2. Data the app accesses

Depending on the features you use, GCube Launcher may access the following data on your device:

- List of installed launchable apps
- Notification listener data needed to show badge counts on supported apps
- App settings and preferences stored locally on your device
- User-selected folder locations used to scan compatible game files for Dolphin integration
- Locally stored app icons, launcher slots, and last selected app information

## 3. How the app uses data

GCube Launcher uses this data only to provide launcher functionality and related user-requested features, including:

- Displaying your installed apps inside the launcher
- Launching apps selected by you
- Showing notification badges when you enable notification access
- Saving launcher preferences such as sound, haptics, startup video, and visual settings
- Saving quick slots, last selected apps, and local launcher state
- Scanning a folder that you explicitly choose for compatible Dolphin game files

## 4. Data collection and transmission

GCube Launcher does **not** create a user account and does **not** require sign-in.

At the time of this policy, GCube Launcher does **not** send your personal data, installed app list, notification data, or selected local folders to a remote backend operated by the developer.

The app uses a local in-app web interface and local device storage to render and power parts of the launcher experience. This local functionality does not mean your data is transmitted to an external server.

## 5. Data sharing

GCube Launcher does **not** sell your personal data.

GCube Launcher does **not** share your personal data with third parties for advertising or data brokerage purposes.

If you choose to launch third-party apps or open external services such as Google Play, those apps or services operate under their own privacy policies.

## 6. Local storage and retention

The app stores certain information locally on your device to preserve functionality and your preferences. This may include:

- Startup video setting
- Sound and haptic settings
- Badge-related launcher state
- Last selected app information
- Pinned launcher slots
- Selected folder URI for Dolphin integration

This data remains on your device until:

- You change it in the app
- You clear app data through Android settings
- You uninstall the app

## 7. Notification access

If you enable notification access, GCube Launcher may read notification listener data required to calculate and display app badge counts inside the launcher. This access is used only for launcher badge functionality and is not sold or shared by the developer.

## 8. Installed apps visibility

GCube Launcher accesses information about installed launchable apps because this is necessary for its core function as a launcher. This information is used only to display and launch apps from the launcher interface and to keep launcher slots up to date.

## 9. Dolphin integration

If you use the Dolphin-related feature, the app accesses only the folder location that you explicitly choose. The app uses this access to scan compatible local files and build a local library view. GCube Launcher does not provide game files, ROMs, BIOS files, or the Dolphin emulator.

## 10. Security

GCube Launcher stores launcher-related data locally on the device. The developer takes reasonable steps to limit data handling to what is necessary for the app's core functionality.

## 11. Children

GCube Launcher is not specifically directed to children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe that the app is handling data in a way that concerns you, please use the contact listed in this policy.

## 12. Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect app changes, platform requirements, or legal requirements. The latest version should be made available wherever the app is distributed.
